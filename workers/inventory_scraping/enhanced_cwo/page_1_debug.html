What Are Aftermarket Wheels?

Simply put, an aftermarket wheel is any wheel that doesn't come on a stock vehicle setup from the factory. Aftermarket wheels come in all shapes, sizes, finishes, and materials, which puts you fully in charge of the customization and the look you want.

If you want to upgrade your rims while keeping the stock appearance there are aftermarket wheel brands that build OE Replica wheels and rims that are a great option too.

How Much Do Aftermarket Wheels Cost?

Depending on the designs, materials used, and brand name, aftermarket wheels can range from $300 to nearly $10,000 for a set.

Don't let this deter you, though, as these are the extreme ends of the spectrum - most sets of custom wheels will go from anywhere between $1000 and $2500, and with the financing options that we offer, you can find a quality set that is guaranteed to fit your truck's vibes and your budget.

Why Should You Buy Aftermarket Truck Wheels?

In most cases, an aftermarket truck wheel is an easy choice to make. Not only do they look badass, but they also provide far more customization for your vehicle than a stock option otherwise would.

For example, say you've got an off-road vehicle in your driveway. A stock wheel can only take you so far because it needs to perform well on the road and track. With an aftermarket set of wheels specifically designed for off-road driving - including Beadlock patterning, tougher construction, etc. - you'll get much more enjoyment out of every ride when you're going mudding, rock crawling, or overlanding.

Will Installing Aftermarket Wheels On Your Truck Void Warranty?

You may hear differently from a dealer, but no, a new set of aftermarket wheels won't void the warranty on your truck or SUV. Where you do need to keep an eye out, though, is with your insurance - if you don't report that you installed new wheels on your vehicle, there might be some issues if you were to incur any damage.

Are Aftermarket Wheels Worth It?

Of course! Building a custom truck is a huge investment, but it's well worth it when you see the finished product. Any enthusiast will tell you that rolling up to your next truck show or swap meet with a badass new ride is one of the coolest feelings on the planet. Pair that with the performance upgrades that new aftermarket wheels can add to your ride, and you've got a winning formula.
